About

BustQue is a leading importer, exporter and distributor of all networking cables, peripherals, equipment and other components essential to building tobust information and telecomunicatins networks. Founded over 17 years ago, the company delivers critical parts and accessoies to ICT sector customers throughout South Africa from its head office in Edenvale, Gauteng. We also supply to countries across the entire African continent.


  • To always provide the very best service and products possible to the client To make realistic promises and over-deliver on those promises.
  • To always conduct business with pride and ethics.
  • To always remain a true BEE company, not only providing work opportunities, but through previously disadvantaged people, managing the company.
  • To treat all people related to the company directly and indirectly with respect and dignity.
    • These simple philosophies have served as a foundation for BustQue to date and will continue to guide us in the coming years. This is evident in the fact that we remain a competitive business, thriving in an industry where only the strongest survive.

“BustQue will become a force to be reckoned with in the distribution of information technology, we will strive become one of the leaders and set the trend in the industry.” By continually setting ourselves new challenges and striving to achieve these, we can only grow from strength to strength. We support and challenge other SMME’s to also grow and contribute to the South African economy

BustQue is a 100% BEE company and it intends on staying that way. This will be done through taking advantage of the Skills Development Act and its provision for learnerships and training. This will be an excellent way for an SMME like BustQue to gain valuable skills and human resources at a somewhat reduced rate. As also previously stated, the company currently has a 50% women’s economic empowerment stake. This is not only substantially higher than the generally accepted norm of 25%, but also significantly higher than the industry norm that is at around 20%. Corporate social investment and corporate responsibility have become buzzwords of late. At BustQue we believe that these should not be done superficially, but rather from a real need and conscience basis. As such, focus has been placed on human issues. These include small charities often overlooked by larger concerns.
